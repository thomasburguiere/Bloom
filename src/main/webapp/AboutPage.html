BLOOM

Workflow for open data curation

The uncertainty and significant biodiversity crisis raise new ecological and environmental questions highlighting the need of monitoring biodiversity. However, the sheer magnitude and complexity of biodiversity data make it impractical to consider the available millions of organismsâ€™ occurrence points for analyzing on a large scale the impact of climate change or addressing macroecological research questions.

Besides the size, the first challenge is the extreme heterogeneity of biodiversity occurrence data. Using occurrence points is non-trivial and curation process should take into account and correct wrong identifications, poor geolocalization, format errors, wrong projection systems, etc. As potentially no single data is the same, it is hardly possible to correct all errors automatically without going through extremely sophisticated validation processes, most of it requiring direct human input and going through each record manually.

Our objective is to provide an efficient and easy tool to improve biodiversity monitoring via an automatic curation procedure of biodiversity data. The BLOOM project will provide a generic solution for biodiversity big data curation process. To this end, we propose a data validation worflow that addresses, for millions of points, simple data quality checking (coordinates, localities, outlier points and taxonomy).
